About Me

DevOps & Backend Developer

I'm a DevOps-focused Backend Developer with hands-on experience in Linux systems, server management, application deployment, and automation. I hold a DevOps Specialization Certificate from IBM, and I'm actively working toward becoming a full-time DevOps Engineer.

Currently, I'm a Research Intern at the Institute of Informatics and Communication (IIC), University of Delhi South Campus, where I manage and deploy secure, scalable systems for academic and government-backed platforms like the SamarthX Project (Ministry of Education, India).

I've worked on configuring Linux servers, deploying web applications using Nginx and Apache, setting up Keycloak for authentication, and managing PostgreSQL and MariaDB databases. I also deploy and customize CMS platforms such as Docusaurus, Grav, CMSimple, and Joomla — often integrating them with backend services and managing domain/host configurations.

In parallel, I'm skilled with containerization and orchestration tools like Docker, Kubernetes, and OpenShift, and I use CI/CD pipelines with GitHub Actions, Travis CI, and automated testing tools like OWASP ZAP and Snyk. I'm also comfortable with monitoring tools like Prometheus and Grafana, and have working knowledge of Chef and Puppet for configuration management.

Whether it's spinning up a fresh server, deploying a secure CMS, or writing clean backend logic, I bring a full-stack mindset with a DevOps foundation — and I'm always looking to automate, optimize, and improve systems wherever possible.
